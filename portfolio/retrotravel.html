Retro Travel Tileset

The retro travel slippy tileset was created using MapBox. This tileset is based upon an old poster that was in my grandparent’s house when I was growing up that now hangs in my parents home. The map has hues of blue, green, and yellow that are represented as accented values in the tileset. In this piece I tried to replicate the travel poster style that was popular in the 70’s and 80’s. I wanted to accentuate all of the main travel features that people look for when travelling, such as, parks, restaurants, trains, and airports. These different categories have icons that I created using Adobe Illustrator and like the map I wanted to keep a retro flowing poster appearance. Some key takeaways from making this map are the sheer amount of complexity that can be added and taken away at a basemap level and how this interplay affects the overall message the map is trying to portray.
